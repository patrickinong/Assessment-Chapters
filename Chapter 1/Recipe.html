Meringue Recipe Tips

Tip 1: For the fluffiest meringue, separate your eggs while they're still cold and let them come to room temperature.

Tip 2: When preparing a meringue, it is important to always use a very clean basin. Should you need to, wash it again just to be sure.

Tip 3: Avoid over-whipping your meringue. It should definitely appear shiny and silky.

Tip 4: Use cream of tartar to help stabilize the meringue to hold its form.

Tip 5: Meringue should be baked for about 10 minutes, or until the peaks have gone golden brown, with the heat source about 4 inches below the surface.

Instructions

Step 1
Since eggs separate best when cold, take them out of the refrigerator and separate.

Step 2
Let the egg whites come to room temperature so they can whip to their maximum potential.

Step 3
Set oven temperature to 350 °F.

Step 4
Transfer egg whites to a well sanitized basin.

Step 5
Add the cream of tartar to the shiny, smooth, and fluffy egg whites to help them maintain their shape.

Step 6
Gradually mix the sugar into the egg whites by adding a tablespoon at a time, beating well after each addition. Beat until stiff peaks form, about 1 minute.

Step 7
Cover the pre-baked or no-bake dessert with meringue, slide it 4 inches beneath the oven, and bake for 10 minutes or until golden.
