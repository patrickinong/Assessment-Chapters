Friends was a successful American comedy on the NBC network from 1994 to 2004. It won six Emmy Awards, including one for Outstanding Comedy Series. From its run until the conclusion, it consistently had a Nielsen rating in the top five or higher, peaking at number one in its eighth season. David Crane and Marta Kauffman wrote the novel Friends, which centers on six young adults living in Greenwich Village, New York City, who are housemates or neighbors. Most of the episode is set while the buddies visit one another at their residences. The show's central themes are the characters' individual and group quests for meaningful relationships, sex, and commitment.
With three men and three women within the group of friends, there are plenty of humorous moments and a wide audience identification due to their diverse personalities and flaws.
